## Story

As a **sprint operator running the orchestrator against a real BMAD project**,
I want the 3-track session history (`.cop1/history/<storyId>/`) to be populated after each session,
so that I have a permanent, human-readable audit trail of what happened during each BMAD command.

## Dev Notes

### Architecture decision

The gap was that `ExchangeHistoryWriter` (EA11-S8) existed but was never called from the orchestrator flow. The `SessionLogger` writes JSONL structured logs; `ExchangeHistoryWriter` writes per-session Track 2 markdown. They are complementary.

The bridge is `SessionInteractionCollector` — a thin subclass of `SessionLogger` that intercepts `logInteraction` calls to collect the full `SessionInteraction` objects in memory. When a session completes, the runner drains the collected interactions and passes them to `ExchangeHistoryWriter.write()`.

This keeps `SessionLogger` unchanged (zero blast radius on existing consumers) while making the interactions available for Track 2.

### Fire-and-forget semantics

Track 2 write failures are caught and logged to console but never fail the session. History is best-effort — the JSONL log (SessionLogger) remains the source of truth.
